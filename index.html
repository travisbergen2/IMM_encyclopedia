A

A(m) Toeplitz Sequence StableEquivalence: conjecture-grade

Paper 14, “The Truncated Weil Form as a Toeplitz Sequence” · doi:10.5281/zenodo.20684022

A re-coordinatization of the Weil explicit formula in a box basis at scale δ, producing a real sequence A(m) = POLE(m) − PRIME(m) + ARCH(m) whose Toeplitz matrices TM = [A(i−j)] encode the Riemann Hypothesis: RH holds if and only if TM ⪰ 0 for every size and every scale (equivalence via Weil's criterion in box coordinates, held at the paper's stated conjecture grade C-14-1). Positive-definite Toeplitz sequences are precisely Fourier coefficients of positive measures on the circle (Herglotz–Carathéodory–Toeplitz), making A(m) intrinsically a circle-side object.

In the pre-registered comparison E-LI-1b the A(m) gauge proved decisively the most sensitive violation detector among the programme's three circle-side meters, resolving 11 of 12 planted-violation cells, each by genuine indefiniteness (μmin < 0). A one-character erratum (δ² → δ in the printed tent transform) and an implicit windowing convention (M = w/δ) were identified during reproduction and are recorded against the paper.

See also Li Criterion and the RH Chart; E-Series Protocols; Weil Positivity.

B

Born Rule from Projection Measures Stage 1: theoremStage 2: proposition under ansatz

Paper 4 of the Foundations Series

A two-stage derivation with explicit labels at each stage. Stage 1 (theorem): three structural conditions on any projection-induced measure — basis invariance, orientation independence, and projection-integration stability — force the measure to be quadratic, μ(αm) = |α|²μ(m), with no Hilbert space assumed. Stage 2 (proposition): under the explicitly load-bearing Hilbert Embeddability Ansatz, polarization yields an inner product and the six Gleason conditions then force P(i) = |⟨i|ψ⟩|² uniquely. The paper states plainly that the Ansatz is not derived, identifies its operational consequences, and names the pathway toward deriving it (Solèr's theorem) as untraveled.

See also Single-Outcome Determinacy; Born-Rule Deviation Test.

Born-Rule Deviation Test Closed form: theorem under six conditionsPrediction: untested

Paper 7 · “Closed Form, Six Forcing Conditions, and a 27-Second Test”

IMM's falsification wager. Six structural conditions uniquely force a specific deviation from the Born rule whose discriminating signature is a sign reversal at exactly PQM = 1/n — a zero no standard decoherence model places there. The positivity bound on the deviation is proved; the coupling ε and scale Δ₀ are declared fit parameters, not derivations. A circuit-QED protocol estimates 27 seconds of transmon time at ε = 10⁻³ would decide it, subject to a stated geometric caveat (⟨F⟩ ≈ 1) that the paper itself flags as requiring verification before any experimental advocacy. If the reversal is absent at 3σ, the prediction at that coupling is falsified — by design.

See also Born Rule from Projection Measures; E-Series Protocols.

C

Claim Ledger Stable · methodological

Paper 0B · the programme's central epistemic instrument

A published register assigning every load-bearing claim in the corpus one of the statuses STABLE, REVISE, OPEN, FALSIFIED, WITHDRAWN, UNTESTED, or CONFIRMED, together with the conditions under which the claim would fail. The ledger's governing maxim — no claim exceeds its derivation — binds all subsequent papers, and every article in this encyclopedia inherits its chip from the ledger's vocabulary.

The ledger is what distinguishes the corpus from the genre it superficially resembles: claims here are killable, and several have been killed by the programme's own instruments (see Retired and Falsified Claims). A full paper-by-paper audit against the ledger's own labels was conducted in July 2026 and is maintained as a living document.

See also Lexicon; E-Series Protocols.

Cosmological Topology Simulation resultGrowth prediction untested

Paper 6 · “Emergent Cosmological Topology from Projection-Integration Dynamics”

Projection-integration dynamics on a 32³ periodic lattice spontaneously produce cosmic-web topology: structure fraction ≈ 30% with deeply negative Euler characteristic (χ = −1602), qualitatively matching survey observations without parameter tuning. A comparison against three reference reaction-diffusion systems places the dynamics in the excitable (fast-activator/slow-inhibitor) universality class — and the paper explicitly replaced its earlier “isomorphism” language with the weaker universality-class statement when the stronger reading failed. The falsifiable residue is a structure-growth exponent (α ≈ 1.063) distinguishable from ΛCDM at the few-percent level, labelled untested against survey data.

Failure Modes of a Receiver Operational taxonomyML predictions: conjecture

Papers 9, 12, 18 · the applied taxonomy of the RPCS-1 Agent Tuner

The receiver-dynamics line identifies characteristic regimes in which a bounded receiver fails: oscillation (gain too high — the receiver chases noise, the oscillatory instability analysed in Paper 9), overload (input rate exceeding integration capacity), and freeze (update rate collapsing to zero — given a derivation as the rational-freeze boundary of the epistemic action-rate law R-3 in Paper 18). Paper 12 conjectures that ML architectures exhibit the same failure modes at structurally corresponding parameter combinations (C-12-1) and derives three falsifiable predictions from the correspondence, all explicitly labelled untested.

Correction note (second edition): the first edition attributed a four-mode classification to a “Theorem 11-2”; no such theorem appears in the published corpus, and the entry has been rewritten from the deposited sources.

G

Gravity as Pushforward Consistency resultAnsätze labelled

Paper 8 · “The IMM Master Action and Its Spectral Extension of General Relativity”

A master action containing the Einstein–Hilbert term alongside spectral-gap and projection-entropy couplings, reducing to general relativity at the physical fixed point. The paper's own honest-scope statement is its most quotable feature: GR is recovered because the Einstein–Hilbert term was included — a consistency construction, “stated explicitly and without apology,” not a derivation from pre-geometric principles. The non-trivial content lies off the fixed point: a dynamical spectral-gap scalar generating a dark-energy analogue with w(z) ≠ −1 (testable against DESI and Euclid), modified structure growth, and the geodesic-pushforward proposition. All three input ansätze are labelled as such in the paper's status map.

H

HIMM Operator Core spectral claims retiredSuccessor question open

Paper 13, “Birman–Schwinger route to RH” · v3

A proposed spectral operator HIMM = H₀ + Vren with Vren(y) = Σp≤P(cos(y log p) − 1)/p, later extended to a det₂-regularized adelic Witten operator, intended to realize the Hilbert–Pólya picture. Independent verification retired its load-bearing numerics: the paper's prime-sum constant (2.636) is in error (the true value over p ≤ 199 is 1.949); the operator's spectral shift function is a dense free-level staircase that does not pin zeta zeros; and the proposed correction knobs were inert (see Retired and Falsified Claims).

Two genuine residues survive the audit: the adelic generator HA on L²(Cℚ) is self-adjoint by Stone's theorem — closing the paper's “Open Problem 4.4” — but its full-space spectrum is all of ℝ, not the zeros; and the honestly open RH-equivalent statement is Weil positivity on the cokernel of the fibre-summation map, which the programme now approaches as instrumentation rather than proof.

See also Weil Positivity; A(m) Toeplitz Sequence; Retired and Falsified Claims.

I

Information Manifold Model (IMM) Programme · open series

T. Bergen, 2025– · Zenodo concept doi:10.5281/zenodo.19697792 · open-ended paper series

A structural research programme spanning quantum foundations, gravitation, receiver dynamics, and number-theoretic instrumentation, published as an open-ended numbered series with three foundation documents: a locked vocabulary (Paper 0A), a public claim ledger (Paper 0B), and a positioning paper situating the framework against rivals — QBism, integrated information theory, active inference, predictive processing — including explicit statements of where IMM has not earned its position (Paper 0C).

The programme's distinguishing commitment is procedural rather than doctrinal: claims are graded, experiments are pre-registered, failures are published, and the series is explicitly labelled open — numbering reflects an ongoing, converging process, not a finished system. Its strongest current assets are the receiver-dynamics line (Papers 9–12 and the Observer Requirements rebuild, Paper 18), which ships as working software, and the number-theoretic instrumentation line (Papers 13, 14, 19 and the E-LI experiments), which maintains an explicit “instrument, not proof” stance toward the Riemann Hypothesis.

See also Claim Ledger; Observer Requirements; Li Criterion and the RH Chart.

Lorentzian Signature PropositionUniqueness gap open

Paper 3 · “Lorentzian Signature as the Stable Informational Valley”

Under IMM's spectral contraction flow, non-Lorentzian deformations of the effective metric decay exponentially (a lemma proved under the spectral boundedness axiom), making Lorentzian signature a stable infrared fixed point. The paper is explicit that its central claim is a Proposition, not a Theorem: stability is proved, uniqueness of the fixed point is not — that step requires a functional-renormalization-group classification which the paper identifies as its single separating gap. The conjecture that fixed-point structure selects dimension four is labelled as depending on that open gap.

Li Criterion and the RH Chart Maps: theorem-gradeInstrument: shipped

Paper 19, “A Smith Chart for the Riemann Hypothesis” · live instrument at travisbergen2.github.io/rh-smith-chart

Li's criterion (1997) rests on the Möbius map z = 1 − 1/s, which sends the critical line to the unit circle: RH is exactly the statement that every nontrivial zero's image lies on the rim, and the Li coefficients λn = Σ(1 − zρn) are power sums of the charted zeros. Paper 18 assembles this with the classical Smith chart of radio-frequency engineering — the identical Cayley fold of positive-real impedances into the disk — into a graded dictionary and a working, tested instrument with three positivity meters (λn, the A(m) Toeplitz gauge, and the Θω inner-function gauge), a planted-violation demonstrator, and a VNA-style swept-trace mode.

The paper's own framing is binding: the chart proves nothing about RH; the underlying mathematics (Möbius geometry, Herglotz functions, de Branges canonical systems, Suzuki's Hamiltonian-positivity criterion) is standard and credited; the contribution is assembly and instrumentation. Its one structural identity: the chart is not an illustration of the Li criterion but its state space.

See also A(m) Toeplitz Sequence; Θω Inner-Function Gauge; Weil Positivity.

M

Market Entity Negative result · published as such

Applied receiver-dynamics experiments (working-thread record, unpublished) · prototypes v1–v3 · verdict recorded 2026

An attempt to instantiate the RPCS-1 receiver as an autonomous trading agent — a predict–act–compare–recalibrate loop with the five primitives given operational trading meanings (integration as evidence bar, thresholding as detection gate, gain as position size, updating as learning rate, anticipation as directional call). The final prototype discriminated predictable from dead regimes (2.6× trade-rate separation) and still lost money: the detectable edge (~1–2 bp/bar) is thinner than realistic costs plus detection lag, and the breakeven autocorrelation (φ ≈ 0.40 at retail cost) does not occur in liquid markets.

The programme's recorded verdict is deliberately blunt: not viable as an alpha engine at retail cost; residual value, if any, as a risk stand-down overlay — a question that was then itself pre-registered (E-OVL-1) rather than assumed. The entry stands in this encyclopedia because published negative results are the cheapest available proof that the grading system is real.

See also E-Series Protocols; RPCS-1 Receiver Primitives.

Paper 12 · cross-substrate conjecture C-12-1

The conjecture that the RPCS-1 stability potential characterises any information-processing receiver — biological or computational — so that transformers at extreme parameter combinations (large attention windows, high head gain, no temperature scaling) should exhibit the same failure modes as biological receivers at corresponding coordinates. P

Projection–Equilibrium Question Theorem A: provedVerdict: corpus does not beat its own null

Paper 16 — Open Series · “A Form-Only Null Hypothesis for the IMM”

The programme's deepest self-test: it constructs the null hypothesis that its own common-source intuition must beat — that recurring structure across the Riemann, Yang–Mills, and signature-selection lines is attributable to shared abstract form alone — and reports that the corpus currently does not beat it. Theorem A (proved) shows that for finite measured quotient systems projection never destroys a spectral gap, so gaps die only in limits; this relocates the corpus's hardness into uniformity tiers, exactly where its experiments independently found it. The paper's continuum identity was found, by its own mandatory literature gate, to be due to Lott (2003); the independent derivation is presented as an alternative proof with credit given. An encyclopedia built to impress would bury this article; this one considers it among the corpus's strongest.

See also Regulated-Positivity Schema; Yang–Mills Mass Gap; Retired and Falsified Claims.

RPCS-1 Receiver Primitives Overlap Theorem: theoremFramework: stable

Paper 9 · primitives TI, SG, FT, UE, AR · identifiability κ = 1.4 (Path-N3)

The five control knobs of a bounded receiver: TI (temporal integration — how much evidence is accumulated), SG (signal gain), FT (filter threshold — how much pressure a signal needs to be noticed), UE (update elasticity), and AR (anticipatory reach), forming a continuous five-dimensional configuration space with a stability potential V(R, E) quantifying receiver–environment mismatch. Paper 9's Overlap Theorem (T-09-1) proves that comorbidity rates in categorical diagnostic systems structurally exceed products of base rates whenever receiver basins overlap — a consequence of projecting a continuum onto discrete labels. Fisher-information analysis confirms all five parameters estimable from the specified assay battery (condition number κ = 1.4, no confound pairs); the V-form and its constants remain labelled ansätze (A-09-1/2/3), and all five predictions are marked untested.

Paper 18 (Observer Requirements) rebuilds the framework's foundations, deriving each primitive as a forced control knob of any bounded estimator–actor; the conjecture that the primitives appear as identifiable dimensions in ML systems belongs to Paper 12 (C-12-1).

See also Observer Requirements; Failure Modes of a Receiver; Measurement Batteries; RPCS-1 Agent Tuner.

Single-Outcome Determinacy Theorem — scope-limited by design

Paper 2 of the Foundations Series

Under the axiom that observation is a many-to-one function Π : M → O, every observation event yields exactly one outcome — a theorem the paper itself calls near-definitional, insisting that its contribution “is not the proof, which is trivial, but the precise scope of what is and is not established.” Determinacy of cardinality (one outcome occurs) is proved; determinacy of identity (which outcome) is explicitly deferred to the projection-measure theory of Paper 4. The measurement problem, in its cardinality form, is dissolved rather than solved.

See also Born Rule from Projection Measures; Temporal Asymmetry.

Θω Inner-Function Gauge Object: established (Suzuki)Gauge law: measured

After Suzuki, arXiv:1204.1827 · implemented in the RH chart; secondary arm of E-LI-1b

The family Θω(z) = ξ(½−ω−iz)/ξ(½+ω−iz), whose meromorphic innerness in the upper half-plane Suzuki tied directly to RH, with explicit canonical systems constructed unconditionally for ω > 1. In network terms Θω is an all-pass filter: unconditionally unimodular on the real axis, with RH governing the absence of poles above it. Implemented as a truncated Blaschke product over the charted zeros, it serves as the instrument's third meter.

E-LI-1b measured its detection law and thereby corrected the registered prediction: the gauge fires precisely when ε − ω ≥ v — the planted pole must reach the probe line — a rule matching 72 of 72 configurations with no false alarms. The gauge is therefore local in both probe coordinates, unlike the two global meters.

See also Li Criterion and the RH Chart; E-Series Protocols.

Temporal Asymmetry Core theorems provedSecond-Law link: conjecture

Paper 1 · “Temporal Asymmetry as a Structural Consequence of Observer Projection”

The arrow of time derived as projection geometry: given a state space of higher dimension than the observer-accessible space, non-trivial dynamics, and a smooth surjective projection, the informational past of an observed state (its preimage fibre) is structurally larger than its informational future (the projected forward image). Three results are proved — genericity of the required non-degeneracy, the entropy inequality Spast > Sfuture via the co-area formula, and dynamical (not merely inferential) irreversibility of the induced dynamics even when the underlying flow is invertible. No appeal to thermodynamics, quantum mechanics, or cosmological boundary conditions; the connections to the Second Law and to experienced temporal passage are labelled conjectures.

See also Single-Outcome Determinacy; Gravity as Pushforward.

Two-Channel Decoherence Two theoremsPredictions untested

Paper 5 · “Two-Channel Decoherence and Passive Coherence Revival”

An open-system model in which a coherent channel and an environmental record exchange amplitude under a projection-pressure field, predicting a three-phase coherence cycle ending in passive revival. Two theorems: total norm is exactly conserved at zero coupling (information transferred to the record is not destroyed), and net revival occurs precisely when the return rate exceeds the projection rate. A computational pre-test confirms no standard Lindblad evolution produces passive revival across three bath spectral types — the two-channel structure is required — and the discriminating prediction is a 1/κ revival-delay scaling, bath-independent at leading order.

See also Born Rule from Projection Measures; E-Series Protocols.

W

Weil Positivity The open frontier · credited

Connes; Connes–Consani (arXiv:2006.13771); Connes–Consani–Moscovici (arXiv:2511.22755)

The genuinely open RH-equivalent statement toward which the programme's number-theoretic line points: positivity of the Weil explicit-formula functional on the cokernel of the fibre-summation map — equivalently, positive-definiteness of the truncated sequence A(m) at all windows and scales, whose boundedness alone is already equivalent to RH by von Koch's estimate. The archimedean part was proved by Connes and Consani (2021); the operator-theoretic frontier is the zeta spectral triples programme (2025).

The encyclopedia records this entry to fix attribution: the deep mathematics here is not IMM's; the programme's contribution is instrumentation of the truncations and the graded dictionary connecting them to network-theoretic positivity.

See also A(m) Toeplitz Sequence; HIMM Operator; Regulated-Positivity Schema.

Y

Yang–Mills Mass Gap Localized, not solved — the paper's first sentence

Paper 15 — Open Series, v3 · “An Orbit-Space Curvature Problem”

The paper's abstract opens by disclaiming what it does not do: it “localizes the forcing half of the Yang–Mills mass gap problem to a single geometric hypothesis and does not solve it.” Within IMM's dictionary (an ansatz, so labelled), the gauge quotient becomes the physical projection and the mass gap follows — conditionally — from uniform positivity of the renormalized Bakry–Émery Ricci curvature on the fundamental modular region. A naïve form of the conjecture is stated, refuted by known regimes (the abelian Göpfert–Mack gap with zero orbit curvature; conformal non-abelian theories), and withdrawn — the refutation producing the surviving hypothesis structure. Four pre-registered lattice experiments instrument the program, including an abelian control in which the gap is correctly attributed to the monopole channel while the curvature observable stays identically zero.

See also Projection–Equilibrium Question; Regulated-Positivity Schema; E-Series Protocols.
